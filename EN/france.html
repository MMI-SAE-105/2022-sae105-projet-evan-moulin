France

An unknown future

Today's France

Today, at my 18th birthday, France is one of the most developed countries in the world and has been for a very long time. It is the country I was born and raised in, and I still want to live there. Although France has had its ups and downs, it is a country that will remain among the greatest and it is one of the countries with the greatest history in the world. I don't doubt for a moment that this country will continue to develop in the future, but I honestly wonder how this country will evolve in the years to come, will all this culture remain in the beautiful country that is France?

The destiny of this country

How will we manage to move forward with new technologies, will nuclear power be the most efficient way to produce energy to supply French homes? How will global warming impact our country? We will be able to answer all these questions in the next few decades. For my part, I want France to remain the country it is today even after all these years, I want it to keep its beautiful landscapes and its historical heritage.

A new world

Despite the fact that it is inevitable, the country will continue to expand its urban landscape and I can already imagine myself in these gigantic cities full of technology with huge buildings. However, this will lead to increased economic development and therefore more jobs. So it's intriguing to imagine what my country France will be like in the future. But in my opinion the future of our country is very promising, so I sincerely hope that I will enjoy the moment when it comes.

The most beautiful monuments of France

1634

The Palace of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles is a castle and historical monument located in Versailles in the Yvelines region of France. It was the main residence of the French kings Louis XIV, Louis XV and Louis XVI.

1758

The Pantheon

The Pantheon is a neo-classical style monument located in the 5ᵉ arrondissement of Paris. In the heart of the Latin Quarter, on the Sainte-Geneviève mountain, it is in the centre of the Place du Panthéon.

1793

The Louvre

The Louvre Museum is a museum located in the 1ᵉʳ arrondissement of Paris, France. A prefiguration of it was imagined in 1775-1776 by the Count d'Angiviller, Director General of the King's Buildings, as a place to display the masterpieces of the Crown's collection.

1806

Arch of triumph

The Arc de Triomphe, is a monument located in Paris, at a high point at the junction of the territories of the 8ᵉ, 16ᵉ and 17ᵉ arrondissements.

1846

Mont saint michel

The architecture of Mont-Saint-Michel and its bay make it the most visited tourist site in Normandy and one of the ten most visited in France.

1887

The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a 330 m high puddled iron tower located in Paris, at the north-western end of the Champ-de-Mars park on the banks of the Seine in the 7ᵉ arrondissement.
